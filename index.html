The Pulse Width Modulation project

The Pulse Width Modulation Project is to design and build the Pulse Width Modulation circuit to control the speed of a DC motor. The circuit design's main idea is to use as less space and as few wires as possible, and the circuit is organized tidily. The wires and devices are connected using soldering. I have also learned to safely use soldering tools and make effective soldering connections.

The House Wiring Project

The house wiring project is to understand how a residential wiring system works and to wire basic devices, such as receptacles, GFCI receptables, three-way switch and light terminals. I have learned to understand the hot, neutral and ground wire connections. I have also learned about the phantom voltage across the three-way switches.

The Panel Wiring Project

The Panel Wiring project is to wire the circuit in a panel according to a schematic diagram. The circuit is to control the a water pumping station. I have learned to understand to read a schematic diagram and install and use devices such as a programmable relay, din rails, terminals, push buttons, two-way switch, pilot lights and power supply.
